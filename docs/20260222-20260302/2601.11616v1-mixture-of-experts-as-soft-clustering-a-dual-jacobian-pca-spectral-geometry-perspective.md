## 摘要
混合专家（MoE）架构通常源于对效率和条件计算的需求，但其对所学函数和表示的几何结构的影响仍缺乏充分刻画。在这项工作中，我们从几何视角研究 MoE，将路由解释为一种将表示空间软划分为重叠局部图谱（local charts）的形式。我们引入了一种双重 Jacobian-PCA 谱几何探测方法，通过 Jacobian 奇异值谱分析局部函数几何，并通过路由隐藏状态的加权 PCA 分析表示几何。利用允许精确计算 Jacobian 的受控 MLP-MoE 设置，我们在容量匹配的情况下对比了稠密（dense）、Top-k 和全软（fully-soft）路由架构。在不同的随机种子下，我们观察到 MoE 路由一致地降低了局部敏感性，与稠密基准相比，专家局部 Jacobian 表现出更小的主奇异值和更快的谱衰减。同时，加权 PCA 表明专家局部表示将方差分布在更多的主要方向上，这表明在相同的输入分布下具有更高的有效秩。我们进一步发现，平均专家 Jacobian 几乎是正交的，这表明变换被分解为低重叠的专家特定子空间，而不是共享映射的缩放变体。我们分析了路由锐度（routing sharpness）如何调节这些效应，结果表明 Top-k 路由产生较低秩、更集中的专家局部结构，而全软路由则产生更宽、更高秩的表示。总之，这些结果支持了将 MoE 视为函数空间软划分的几何解释，这种划分在重新分布表示方差的同时平滑了局部曲率。

## Abstract
Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architectures are commonly motivated by efficiency and conditional computation, but their effect on the geometry of learned functions and representations remains poorly characterized. In this work, we study MoEs through a geometric lens, interpreting routing as a form of soft partitioning of the representation space into overlapping local charts. We introduce a Dual Jacobian-PCA Spectral Geometry probe. It analyzes local function geometry via Jacobian singular-value spectra and representation geometry via weighted PCA of routed hidden states. Using a controlled MLP-MoE setting that permits exact Jacobian computation, we compare dense, Top-k, and fully-soft routing architectures under matched capacity. Across random seeds, we observe that MoE routing consistently reduces local sensitivity, with expert-local Jacobians exhibiting smaller leading singular values and faster spectral decay than dense baselines. At the same time, weighted PCA reveals that expert-local representations distribute variance across a larger number of principal directions, indicating higher effective rank under identical input distributions. We further find that average expert Jacobians are nearly orthogonal, suggesting a decomposition of the transformation into low-overlap expert-specific subspaces rather than scaled variants of a shared map. We analyze how routing sharpness modulates these effects, showing that Top-k routing produces lower-rank, more concentrated expert-local structure, while fully-soft routing yields broader, higher-rank representations. Together, these results support a geometric interpretation of MoEs as soft partitionings of function space that flatten local curvature while redistributing representation variance.
